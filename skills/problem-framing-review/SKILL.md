# Problem framing review

## Purpose

Use [`references/discovery-artifact.md`](references/discovery-artifact.md) when creating or exchanging structured discovery records.

Help stakeholders inspect how a problem frame was derived and make an explicit readiness decision. Alignment means shared understanding of evidence and disagreements, not universal agreement.

## Workflow

1. **Define the decision.** State whether the group is reviewing accuracy, choosing among hypotheses, authorizing more discovery, or selecting a next experiment.
2. **Share before meeting.** Provide the verbatim request, evidence map, candidate hypotheses, contradictions, assumptions, and open questions with citations.
3. **Review evidence first.** Ask whether sources are represented faithfully before debating interpretations or solutions.
4. **Invite correction and dissent.** Ask affected groups what is missing, overstated, or different in their context. Give minority perspectives explicit space.
5. **Compare frames.** Discuss which evidence each hypothesis explains, where it fails, and what decision risk remains.
6. **Test readiness.** Determine whether uncertainty is acceptable for the next action’s cost and reversibility—not whether everything is known.
7. **Record the decision.** Choose proceed, revise, gather targeted evidence, run a reversible experiment, defer, or decline. Record owner, rationale, dissent, assumptions accepted, and review date.
8. **Confirm the record.** Give participants a chance to correct the written outcome.

## Facilitation prompts

- “Which evidence do you interpret differently?”
- “Whose experience is missing?”
- “What would have to be true for this frame to be wrong?”
- “Are we aligned on the evidence, the interpretation, or only the next action?”
- “Which uncertainty is unacceptable for this decision?”

## Guardrails

- Do not summarize silence as agreement.
- Do not weight a view solely by organizational rank.
- Do not force consensus when a decision owner can proceed while recording dissent.
- Do not hide uncertainty to make the brief appear complete.
- Do not let solution debate replace review of the problem evidence.

## Completion checklist

- [ ] Participants reviewed source-linked evidence
- [ ] Corrections and dissent are recorded
- [ ] Decision and owner are explicit
- [ ] Accepted risks and assumptions are visible
- [ ] Next action has a completion or revisit condition
- [ ] Participants can correct the decision record
